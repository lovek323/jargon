\mainentry{benchmark} n.

[techspeak] An inaccurate measure of computer performance. ``In the computer
industry, there are three kinds of lies: lies, damn lies, and benchmarks.''
Well-known ones include Whetstone, Dhrystone, Rhealstone (see \citeentry{h}),
the Gabriel LISP benchmarks (see \citeentry{gabriel}), the SPECmark, suite, and
LINPACK. See also \citeentry{machoflops}, \citeentry{MIPS}, \citeentry{smoke and
mirrors}.
