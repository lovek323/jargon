\mainentry{blink} vi.,n.

[now rare] To use a navigator or off-line message reader to minimize time spent
on-line to a commercial network service (a necessity in many places outside the
US\opt{changes}{\footnote{This was `U.S.' in the original, but I changed it to
US for consistency with the other terms in this paragraph and throughout the
rest of the book.}} where the telecoms monopolies charge per-minute for local
calls). As of late 1994, this term was said to be in wide use in the UK, but is
rare or unknown in the US. In early 2000 it was reported that the term had
apparently passed out of use in the UK\opt{changes}{\footnote{This was `U.K.' in
the original.}}.
